## Problem Section

**Section label:** `THE BOTTLENECK`

### Most Contract Manufacturers Are Built for Volume. Your Prototype Isn't.

Early-stage medical device development doesn't follow a linear path — it cycles. You validate a concept, find a flaw, redesign overnight, and need the next iteration in your hands before your investor meeting or your IRB submission. Standard contract manufacturers quote in weeks, require production-level drawings, and charge tooling fees before they've touched your file.

That's not a manufacturing partner. That's a delay.

**Common friction points this audience faces:**

- Long quoting processes that assume finalized engineering drawings already exist
- MOQ requirements that force 500-unit orders when 5 parts are needed for validation
- Material limitations that compromise mechanical properties during critical testing phases
- No design feedback — vendors who print exactly what was submitted, even when a 2mm wall change would prevent delamination under load

## Process Section

**Section label:** `HOW IT WORKS`

### Four Steps From Concept to Production-Intent Part

1. **Upload files or a design brief** — STL, STEP, or a plain description of what the part needs to do. We meet teams where their design actually is.
2. **Review and response within 24 hours** — DFM feedback, material recommendation, lead time, and quote. No black-box pricing.
3. **Approval and build** — Production runs in 3–7 business days depending on process and volume. Progress updates provided, not silence.
4. **Parts arrive ready for testing** — Post-processed, finished, and documented. Ready for bench testing, clinical validation, or investor demonstration.
